Tribute to cesear chavez

Civil Rights Activists

timeline

  • 1927

    March 31, 1927 Cesar Estrada Chavez was born near Yuma, Arizona.

  • 1942

    Chavez begins as a farmworker.

  • 1946

    Chavez begins military service in the U.S. Navy, which last 2 years.

  • 1952

    He becomes an organizer for the Community Service Organization (CSO) a latino right group.

  • 1958

    Chavez becomes CSO's national director.

  • 1962

    Chavez left the CSO and co-founded the National Farm Workers Association (NFWA) with Delores Huerta. It was later called the United Farm Workers (UFW).

  • 1966

    The NFWA led strike of California grape pickers on a historic march from Delano to California state capitol in Sacramento.

  • 1968

    In February, Chavez begins his historic 25 day fast.

  • 1969

    UFW declares National Grape Boycott Day.

  • 1970

    In December, Chavez is imprisoned for challenging injunction against the boycott.

  • 1973

    UFW celebrates first convention in Fresno.

  • 1975

    California Supreme Court declares the short-handled hoe an unsafe hand tool thus banned by California law.

    •
  • 1977

    An agreement was reached that gave the UFW the sole right to organize farm workers.

  • 1984

    Chavez announces a new grape boycott, this time focused on pesticides.

  • 1988

    Chavez fasts for 36 days to protest pesticide use.

  • 1993

    In April 23, after a fast of several days, Chavez dies in his sleep of unkown cause.
